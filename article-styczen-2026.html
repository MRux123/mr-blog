Global Events, Diplomacy, and Pop Culture

The year opened with the 2026 Winter Olympic Games in Milan and Cortina d'Ampezzo, featuring the Olympic debut of ski mountaineering as a new discipline[cite: 134, 138]. Geopolitically, the New START treaty between the USA and Russia expired, ending a key arms control regime and sparking a new phase in the arms race[cite: 139, 140]. In Iran, exiled Crown Prince Reza Pahlavi called for mass mobilisation, leading to millions protesting and a brutal government crackdown[cite: 141, 142]. Donald Trump held unsuccessful nuclear talks with Iran in Geneva and Oman[cite: 143]. In Europe, Bulgaria officially adopted the euro, becoming the 21st member of the eurozone[cite: 144]. Pop culture celebrated the return of BTS from military service with a new album and world tour announced, as well as the announcement of The Rolling Stones' 25th studio album[cite: 145, 146].

Poland: Economic Growth and New Systems

Poland established itself as a regional leader, posting a 2025 GDP growth of 3.6%—the highest among large EU economies (against the EU average of 1.6%)[cite: 135, 149]. This was driven by rising investment, strong consumption, and a 6% year-on-year increase in real wages, with 2026 forecasts pointing to 3.4–3.7% growth (OECD/NBP)[cite: 150, 153]. On January 1, the central NFZ e-registration system launched to cut waiting times and simplify appointment booking, and the deposit return system (kaucyjny) for beverage packaging came fully into force[cite: 151, 152]. The Warsaw Stock Exchange enjoyed a strong start across large, mid, and small caps, and the Polish zloty strengthened[cite: 158]. Politically, President Nawrocki kept his electoral promise by declining to host Hanukkah celebrations at the Presidential Palace, escalating tensions with the coalition after 17 vetoes and 117 signed documents[cite: 135, 155, 156]. Internal tensions also threatened to split the Poland 2050 (Holownia) coalition[cite: 157].

Winter Sports and Competitions

Polish athletes fought for podiums as the Winter Olympics awarded its first medals[cite: 160].
