The Escalating War and Global Fallout

The first week of March was entirely dominated by the ongoing US-Israeli war with Iran[cite: 202, 205]. The Strait of Hormuz remained closed, destabilising global energy markets, causing oil prices to surge, and triggering panic in financial markets where risk premiums on emerging markets, including Poland, widened sharply[cite: 203, 238, 239]. The EU accelerated supply diversification and intensified negotiations with alternative oil and gas producers[cite: 240]. Militarily, the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps confirmed that Iran struck US bases in the UAE and Kuwait[cite: 206]. Tensions within NATO rose when an Iranian missile was intercepted by NATO air defences over Turkey, prompting US Deputy Secretary of Defense Elbridge Colby to warn that Iran was prepared to strike NATO member states[cite: 208, 209]. Russia actively shared intelligence with Iran, drawing US public condemnation, while the CIA supported Kurdish armed groups inside Iran, risking drawing Turkey into the conflict and fracturing NATO cohesion[cite: 210, 212]. German Chancellor Friedrich Merz warned of uncontrollable long-term consequences, stating: "We do not want a Syria scenario"[cite: 213]. Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian asserted Iran's "obligation and legitimate right" to retaliate, and a timeline was set for selecting a new Supreme Leader following Khamenei's death[cite: 214, 215]. Preliminary casualty figures indicated thousands killed and wounded[cite: 216]. Another country deployed troops toward the conflict zone, and a frigate was dispatched to Cyprus[cite: 217]. Meanwhile, Swedish authorities detained a suspicious vessel in the Baltic Sea linked to Russian incidents, and a series of explosions across Europe pointed to Russian intelligence services (with Polish investigators involved)[cite: 218, 243]. Lithuanian intelligence reported that Russia may be planning aggression against the Baltic region once the Ukrainian conflict is frozen[cite: 244]. Tragically, a Polish pilot was killed in an aviation accident near Chicago[cite: 219].

Poland: Evacuations and Election Strategy

The Polish Foreign Ministry and Radoslaw Sikorski coordinated the evacuation of Polish citizens from Iran and the wider region, confirming the embassy staff were safe[cite: 204, 222, 223, 224]. The Defence Minister requested a sanction for a PiS MP over scandalous remarks about the strikes[cite: 225]. Domestically, PiS held a party convention in Krakow, unveiling their 2027 election strategy and naming Przemyslaw Czarnek as the prime ministerial candidate[cite: 227, 228]. Opinion polls showed KO leading with 189 seats against PiS's 150, with Grzegorz Braun gaining ground as the Confederation lost support, and hypothetical presidential polling showing dramatic results for one former candidate[cite: 230, 231]. The Presidential Chancellery invited the government for talks regarding the SAFE programme and set a meeting date[cite: 204, 229]. On the economic side, the NBP reference rate held at 3.75% as consumer price stabilisation continued[cite: 241].

Sport, Pop Culture, and the Environment

As the Winter Olympics approached its close, Poland's final medal tally took shape, and ski jumper Dawid Kubacki fought for victory in Lillehammer in the World Cup standings[cite: 233, 234]. In culture, Bad Bunny was announced as the headliner of the first-ever concert at Warsaw's PGE Narodowy stadium, and Lukasz Palkowski's psychological drama "Pojedynek" (The Duel) premiered on February 27[cite: 235, 236]. Environmentally, Saharan dust passed over Poland, attracting widespread media attention[cite: 245].
