Global Ceasefires and Supply Chain Shocks

On October 10, a ceasefire between Israel and Hamas took effect in the Gaza Strip[cite: 47, 52]. Although fighting ceased, the humanitarian crisis persisted, with around 1.6 million residents facing famine[cite: 53]. By October 22, the International Court of Justice called on Israel to ensure humanitarian access, as aid organisations warned of severe obstructions[cite: 54, 55]. During the APEC Summit in Busan, Donald Trump and Xi Jinping met to discuss China's controversial new export controls on rare earth metals[cite: 48, 56, 57]. These controls, requiring a Chinese licence for any product containing even 1% of the materials, severely disrupted global supply chains[cite: 57, 58]. Elsewhere, the Czech Republic's parliamentary elections were won by a centre-right coalition, while Romania's presidential election was marred by scandals and street protests[cite: 59, 60]. In Ukraine, a Russian strike caused a blackout in the Chernobyl 'Ark' zone[cite: 61]. Meanwhile, EU leaders met in Copenhagen and agreed to build a 'drone wall' along Europe's eastern border[cite: 62].

Poland: Anti-Corruption and Security Policies

Polish politics were shaken when Jacek Sutryk, Mayor of Wroclaw, faced corruption charges, prompting the ruling Civic Coalition to demand his resignation[cite: 49, 65]. Simultaneously, the Constitutional Accountability Commission held hearings regarding NBP Governor Adam Glapinski's potential responsibility before the Tribunal of State[cite: 49, 66]. The government reviewed the Civil Protection and Civil Defence Programme to procure emergency equipment and drafted a law on the Security and Defence Fund, backed by approximately 4.65 billion euros from the RRF/KPO[cite: 67, 68]. Legal changes included a September 9 amendment to the Developer Act regarding liability for construction defects[cite: 69]. On the security front, Interior Minister Marcin Kierwinski met Baltic state interior ministers in Gdansk, and a presidential decision officially authorised the military to support the Border Guard[cite: 71, 72].

Economy: Deficits and Discoveries

The US imposed new sanctions on Russian oil, further tightening restrictions on the Russian economy[cite: 74]. At the same time, the US faced a record budget deficit of $284.3 billion in October alone, surpassing peak Covid-19 figures[cite: 77]. While gold stabilised and silver held near multi-year peaks, Poland received significant economic news with the discovery of large copper deposits, boosting the domestic mining industry amid the global focus on Chinese rare earth export controls[cite: 75, 76, 78].
