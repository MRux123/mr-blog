## WORLD

*   **Escalation in the Middle East:** The conflict involving Israel, Iran, and Hezbollah has reached a critical stage, with the Lebanese health ministry reporting 850 deaths following an Israeli offensive. Simultaneously, the U.S. is pressuring international allies to secure the Strait of Hormuz, as the closure of this vital oil transit route continues to drive global energy price uncertainty.
*   **Humanitarian Concerns in Conflict Zones:** Increased military operations in both Lebanon and the occupied West Bank have led to significant civilian casualties. The international community is closely monitoring these developments, which have been compounded by a fatal U.S. military plane crash in Iraq during ongoing anti-Iran operations.
*   **Cultural Restitution:** In a landmark move for post-colonial diplomacy, France has officially returned the sacred "Djidji Ayôkwé" talking drum to Côte d'Ivoire, ending over a century of foreign possession and marking a significant step in the ongoing global dialogue regarding the repatriation of looted cultural artifacts.

## POLAND

*   **Protests Against Presidential Veto:** Thousands of demonstrators gathered in front of the Presidential Palace in Warsaw to protest against President Karol Nawrocki’s decision not to sign the "SAFE" act. The legislation, which is tied to critical EU defense funding, has become a flashpoint for political division, with protesters accusing the administration of jeopardizing national security.
*   **Criminal Investigation of Former Footballer:** Polish authorities have launched a nationwide search for Kamil Sylwestrzak, a former Ekstraklasa footballer. The 37-year-old is wanted on serious charges, including heading an organized crime group and involvement in large-scale drug trafficking.
*   **Tragic Rail Accident:** A fatal collision occurred at an unguarded level crossing in Ogorzelice, where a BMW was struck by a railbus. The incident resulted in one death and highlights ongoing concerns regarding safety standards at non-monitored railway crossings in the region.
