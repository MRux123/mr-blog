Middle East Turmoil and Global Incidents

December witnessed mass anti-government demonstrations in Iran, erupting on December 28 amid a currency collapse and economic crisis, quickly spreading to all provinces and continuing into 2026[cite: 103, 106, 107]. In Moscow, Russian General Fanil Sarvarov was killed in a car bombing, while a Russian An-22 military transport aircraft crashed in Ivanovo Oblast, leaving 7 dead[cite: 108, 112]. Russia also fired missiles at the Odessa port area, killing at least 8 and wounding 27[cite: 109]. A series of explosions across Europe prompted investigations pointing to Russian intelligence services[cite: 110]. In Ireland, further remains of children were discovered at the former Mother and Baby Home site in Tuam[cite: 111].

Poland: Presidential Vetoes and Corporate Changes

President Nawrocki vetoed the Crypto-Assets Act, bringing his cumulative veto tally to 17[cite: 102, 115]. The government faced public backlash after leaked correspondence from the Ministry of Health to the Finance Ministry revealed proposed cuts of 10.4 billion PLN, including limits on specialist referrals and restrictions on free medicines for children and seniors[cite: 102, 116]. Maciej Duszczyk stepped down as Deputy Minister of the Interior[cite: 117]. Corporately, UOKiK fined Jeronimo Martins Polska (Biedronka) nearly 105 million PLN for misleading consumers, and PKP Cargo CEO Agnieszka Wasilewska-Semail was removed from the board[cite: 118, 122]. The Ministry of National Defence acquired the Huta Czestochowa steelworks in a strategic move[cite: 119]. In the Church, Cardinal Grzegorz Rys assumed office as Archbishop Metropolitan of Krakow with a solemn investiture at Wawel Cathedral[cite: 102, 121].

Sport, Economy, and Technology

In the sporting world, Lando Norris won his first Formula 1 World Drivers' Championship, securing the Constructors' Championship for McLaren[cite: 103, 124]. In chess, Magnus Carlsen won the World Rapid Chess Championship in Doha, while Alexandra Goryachkina and Bibisara Assaubayeva triumphed in the Women's Rapid and Blitz championships, respectively[cite: 125, 126]. The Four Hills Tournament 2025/2026 commenced as Polish ski jumpers prepared for the season opener[cite: 127]. Economically, Poland maintained robust growth, with Q3 GDP up 3.8% year-on-year and investment rising 7.1%[cite: 129]. While scientists globally reported milestones in nuclear fusion, the Russian economy suffered under Western sanctions following continued attacks on civilians[cite: 130, 131]. Domestically, Poczta Polska stabilised its finances but announced collective redundancies for 2026[cite: 132].
